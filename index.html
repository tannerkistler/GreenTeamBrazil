Overview

For many years, citizens in Brazil have had poor access to quality healthcare, especially when evaluated and compared to the standards set by Target 3.8 of the Sustainable Development Goals.Brazil's Unified Health System (Sistema Único de Saúde) is one of the worlds largest public heatlhcare systems and has made efforts to improve coverage. However, there are still significant gaps remaining in both quality, equity, and accessibility.

When compared to Target 3.c, Brazil also faces challenges in financing healthcare and maintaining a good workforce. Currently, Brazil's medical workforce is limited by underfunded hospitals, shortage of proper equipment, and lack of overall resources. Trained professionals concetrate in wealthier areas, resulting in the incapability to deliver high-quality healthcare services across the whole country. Recruiting, training, and retaining workers still remains insufficent.

Our Mission

Everyone deserves fair and easy access to affordable, quality healthcare. We strive to inform the general public about the social issues regarding the public's healthcare issues with hopes to inform others about the ongoing problems.
